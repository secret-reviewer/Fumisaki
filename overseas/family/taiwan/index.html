Family Log Entry #001

Taiwan: Nine In A Van

Nine people. One 9-seater. One designated driver. Yilan's countryside farms and Taipei's city grind packed into eight days — with an elderly in tow, three kids running on unlimited energy, and zero margin for navigation errors on roads I've never driven before.

// SR-FAMILY-002 // Yilan + Taipei, Taiwan // Jun 2025

Mission Overview

Crew
5 Adults + 1 Elderly + 3 Kids (9 pax total)
Vehicle
9-Seater van — solo driver (me)
Phase 1
14–17 Jun — Yilan (4 nights, DIY farms + night markets)
Phase 2
18–21 Jun — Taipei (4 nights, sightseeing + MRT days)
Flight Out
14 Jun — Scoot TR874, SG T1 → Taoyuan T1
Flight Back
22 Jun — Scoot TR899, Taoyuan T1 → SG T1
Car Return
19 Jun, 7PM — before switching to public transport

The Driver's Perspective

Every road trip has its navigator, its entertainment director, its resident complainer about rest stops. I was none of those. I was the one moving nine people across Taiwan's coastal highways and mountain-adjacent roads in a van, with an elderly passenger to consider on every turn, three kids who needed bathroom breaks that didn't align with any sensible route, and GPS input that had to happen before moving — because curvy roads and last-minute reroutes with a 9-seater don't mix.

Phase 1 in Yilan was the easier half — relatively clear roads, spacious carparks, and most attractions had enough space to manoeuvre the van. Phase 2 heading into Taipei was a different calculation: the coastal route in was scenic but long, Jiufen's roads are narrow, and city driving with this size of vehicle requires a different mental model entirely. We returned the car on Day 6 evening and switched to MRT and taxis for the last two days — best decision of the whole trip.

// Phase 01 — Yilan
DAY 1 14 Jun (Sat) — Arrival
SG → Taoyuan → Yilan

Early departure from Singapore — 6AM leave for Changi T1. Flight TR874 boards at 9:45AM, touchdown Taoyuan International T1 at 2:30PM local time. Cleared customs and collected the 9-seater by 4PM. First time behind the wheel of a right-hand-drive van on a road system I'd only seen on Google Maps — took a few minutes to calibrate, then we moved.

Two-hour drive to Yilan with the full crew aboard. Everyone in their seats, luggage loaded, elderly up front for comfort and easier access. Reached the hotel by 6PM, dumped all luggage, and immediately went out before anyone could decide they were too tired. Dongmen Night Market was the right call for Day 1 — takeaway dinner, no pressure, easy crowd. Everyone eats, everyone's happy, back at the hotel by 8PM.

// Driver note: Taoyuan → Yilan is mostly freeway. Straightforward. The hotel carpark fits the van — verify this before booking any accommodation with a 9-seater.

DAY 2 15 Jun (Sun) — DIY Day 1
Brick Art · Taxi Museum · Onion Farm · Night Market

Breakfast at 8AM, on the road by 9:30AM. First stop: Brick Art Museum ↗ Maps — 30 minutes from the hotel. The kids build special LEGO heart lamps in a DIY session. This is the kind of activity where the adults have just as much fun watching as the kids do building. Allocate at least an hour.

Lunch at noon, then across to the Taxi Museum ↗ Maps — 20 minutes away. More interesting than it sounds. A lot of taxi-related displays and memorabilia; weirdly engaging for everyone, kids included. Worth 1–1.5 hours.

By 3:30PM we were at 蔥寶寶體驗農場 (Onion Baby Farm) ↗ Maps — DIY scallion pancake making, about 1.5 hours of activity. The elderly is comfortable here — it's ground-level, not rushed, and the pancake-making is something everyone can participate in at their own pace. Practical food souvenir: you eat what you make.

Luodong Night Market at 5PM ↗ Maps for dinner and street food. Back at the hotel 6–7PM.

DAY 3 16 Jun (Mon) — DIY Day 2
Crayon Factory · Coral Museum · Lake Ride

Breakfast 8AM, depart 9:30AM. Lucky Art Crayon Factory ↗ Maps — hands-on crayon-making session, expect to be here 4–6 hours. This one's a long stop on purpose. The kids are occupied with something creative and genuinely fun, which means the adults can breathe. Budget the full morning and into early afternoon.

From 2PM: 綺麗珊瑚博物館 (Qili Coral Museum) ↗ Maps — retro displays, outdoor playground for the kids, and the kind of place that'll hold the attention of one particular adult in the group who appreciates anything decorative and vintage. Could stretch to 5PM easily.

If time allows: 梅花湖 (Plum Blossom Lake) ↗ Maps — electric bicycle loop around the lake, closes 5PM. About 40 minutes from the hotel. Worth it if you get out of the museum before 4PM. Note that the elderly may prefer to sit this one out at the lakeside rather than cycling — plan accordingly.

Night market for dinner — either Luodong or Dongmen, whichever the group hasn't had enough of yet.

DAY 4 17 Jun (Tue) — DIY Day 3 + Pack Up
Mr Brown · Rabbit Pencil School · Zhang Mei Farm

Last full day in Yilan. Breakfast 8AM, out by 9:30AM. Coffee stop first: 伯朗咖啡館 城堡一門市 (Mr Brown Coffee Castle) ↗ Maps — 25 minutes north. Strong views, castle aesthetics, good coffee. Short stop, mostly for the scenery and a proper sit-down before the activities start.

Optional detour: Yilan Cultural and Creative Park ↗ Maps at 11:30AM — retro buildings, worth 30 minutes if you're ahead of schedule. Skip if time is tight.

12:15PM: Rabbit Pencil School ↗ Maps — make your own pencil. 1.5–2 hours. This is a must. The kids understand what "making something from scratch" means after this one. Compact activity, very hands-on, and the end product is something they'll actually keep.

2:30PM: Zhang Mei Grandma Farm Mini Zoo ↗ Maps — 15 minutes from the pencil school. Pure zoo visit is about 1 hour. If the group wants the DIY baking option, that adds another 3 hours and you won't leave until 5:30PM — factor that into tonight's dinner plan. Reminder: tonight is pack-up night. Check-out tomorrow morning. Get the bags sorted before sleeping.

// Backup Yilan attractions if schedule slips ahead: Luodong Forestry Culture Park (free, retro trains) · 水岸森林物語 mini zoo NT200/person · 星夢森林劇場 mini zoo NT250/person · National Centre for Traditional Arts NT150/person

// Phase 02 — Taipei
DAY 5 18 Jun (Wed) — Transit + Jiufen
Yinyang Sea · Jiufen · Hotel Check-in · Shilin

Check-out from Yilan, bags loaded, full crew aboard by 9:30AM. Today is the transit day — coastal highway from Yilan up towards Taipei, with two stops on the way.

11:30AM: 陰陽海 (Yinyang Sea) ↗ Maps — a short visual stop. The sea here splits into two distinct colours due to mineral runoff from the old copper mines above. Pull over, photo stop, maybe 20–30 minutes. The elderly can stay in the van if the path is difficult — the view is visible from the roadside.

12:30PM: 九份 (Jiufen) ↗ Maps. Important driver note on this one: input the next 3 destinations into GPS before leaving, and set routes to avoid curvy mountain roads where possible. Jiufen's access roads are narrow — a 9-seater here requires patience and confidence. Carparks fill up fast. Arrive before 1PM if possible. Lunch and explore the old streets, tea houses, and stairways. The lanterns and hillside views are worth the drive complexity. Budget 2–3 hours. The elderly will appreciate it most from the lower sections — the upper stairs are steep. Keep that in mind when managing the group's movement.

Leave Jiufen by 3:30PM, arrive at Roaders Plus Hotel Theme Taipei Station ↗ Maps by 4:30PM. Check in, drop luggage, brief rest before the evening. 5:45PM: Shilin Night Market ↗ Maps — biggest night market in Taipei, best to arrive early before the weekend crowd peaks. Dinner, snacks, oyster omelettes. Back at the hotel by 7PM.

DAY 6 19 Jun (Thu) — Tamsui + Car Return
Tamsui · Snoopy Centre · Astronomical Museum · Return Car

Last day with the van. Breakfast 8AM, move off 9:30AM. Tamsui Fisherman's Wharf ↗ Maps — open waterfront area, seafood stalls, boardwalk. Good for the elderly — flat, accessible, easy pacing. About 2 hours here. The kids run around the waterfront while the adults eat.

12:30PM: Move off. 1:15PM: Snoopy Play Centre ↗ Maps — 2 hours of supervised kids' playtime. This is a deliberate buffer slot: the adults get a break, the kids get to run, and it ticks an activity box without needing anyone to manage a museum or farm. Good call mid-trip.

Next door after Snoopy: Taipei Astronomical Museum ↗ Maps from 4PM. Planetarium vibes, space exhibits, interactive displays. Good for a 60–90 minute wind-down before heading back.

5:30PM: Drive back to hotel, drop everyone off. 7PM: Return the van. ↗ Drop-off point — after this, we're on MRT and taxis for the remaining days. Strategically the right call: Taipei's city streets with a 9-seater and diminishing parking options isn't worth the stress when public transport covers everything cleanly.

DAY 7 20 Jun (Fri) — Public Transport Day
Ximending · Taipei 101 · Raohe Night Market

Car is gone. Today we move like locals. Breakfast 8AM, out by 9:30AM. Bus to Ximending — stop located at Jih Sun International Bank, take Bus 252 富德 direction, 2 stops. Arrive around 10AM.

Ximending ↗ Maps — pedestrianised shopping and street food district. Compact enough to manage as a group on foot. Fashion, snacks, arcades, the works. Keep the kids in sight here — it gets busy. Budget 2–3 hours.

1PM: MRT from Ximending → Bannan Line → Platform 1 direction Taipei City Hall → 7 stops to Taipei 101 area → 40 minutes total. Taipei 101 ↗ Maps at 1:40PM — observation deck, shopping floors, the full experience. Allocate 2–3 hours minimum. The elderly may prefer the indoor floors over the outdoor deck depending on weather.

4:30PM: Taxi to Raohe Night Market — NT165, about 3km away, awkward on public transport. Easier to just cab it. Night market dinner, street food, herbs, medicinal egg stalls, the atmosphere. Back at the hotel 6–7PM.

DAY 8 21 Jun (Sat) — Final Full Day
TAMED FOX · Pokémon Center · Houtong Cat Village

9AM move off. 9:30AM: Breakfast at TAMED FOX 信義店 ↗ Maps — set the tone right for the last real day. Brunch-style, good food, proper table time before the day starts moving fast.

11AM: Pokémon Center Taipei ↗ Maps opens at 11AM sharp. If the kids are into Pokémon, this is the highlight of the whole trip for them. Merch, plushies, limited edition items. Budget accordingly — wallets will be tested. One hour minimum.

Noon: MRT from Taipei City Hall → Bannan Line Platform 1 → 4 stops to Nangang → change to local train 4182 direction Su Ao → 10 stops, 45 minutes → Houtong Cat Village ↗ Maps. Arrive around 1:30PM.

A village literally run by cats. The cats are semi-feral but friendly enough — the kids will lose their minds. The elderly can find a bench and watch the chaos. Good 2-hour stop. Leave by 3:30PM: back to Nangang station, transfer to train 4021 to Shulin → 5 stops, 60 minutes → Taipei Station. Reach hotel around 5PM. Pack up tonight — flight tomorrow. Dinner around the hotel area or nearby.

// Note on skipped attractions: Shifen Waterfall and Yehliu Ocean World were considered but cut — both are 2 hours one way, and the taxi fare to Yehliu runs up to NT2000. Not worth it with nine people and a packed schedule.

DAY 9 22 Jun (Sun) — Departure
Hotel → Taoyuan T1 → SG T1

Last breakfast. Check-out. Airport taxi booked via www.tripool.app — book in advance, not the morning of. For a 9-seater group: van booking, pricing approximately NT1,800 (~SGD70) one way. Leave for Taoyuan by 12:30PM. Flight TR899 departs at 4:30PM from Terminal 1. Land Changi T1 at 9PM Singapore time. Trip closed.

Driver's Debrief

Running a 9-seater with mixed ages across two different zones in Taiwan is a specific kind of logistics puzzle. The Yilan half is forgiving — wide roads, clear signage, farm-style carparks. Taipei is where you earn it: narrow heritage streets, city carparks with height restrictions, and GPS that occasionally insists on the most scenic but least practical route.

  • Pre-load GPS before every leg. Do it at the hotel, before the engine starts. With 8 other opinions in the van, trying to input addresses while moving is a recipe for wrong turns and family tension.
  • Jiufen's roads are genuinely narrow. 9-seater needs full focus, low speed, and a clear plan for which carpark you're targeting before you start climbing.
  • Return the car on Day 6. This was the right call. Taipei city core with a large van and paid carparks is an unnecessary stress when MRT covers every major destination cleanly.
  • Elderly seating: front passenger, always. Easier to board, smoother ride, less motion sickness risk on winding coastal roads. The back rows get the road's full movement — don't put the elderly there.
  • Fuel up at designated stations, not just convenience pumps. Confirm van fuel type before you drive off the rental lot. Don't assume.

Two hours of unmanaged kid energy spent productively. 1600 HRS: Taipei Astronomical Museum next door. Planetarium show + exhibits. Good cool-down activity. 1730 HRS: Return to hotel. Everyone dropped. 1900 HRS: Car returned. That chapter closed. No damage, full tank, clean handover. Weight off the shoulders. From this point: MRT + taxi for remainder of trip.

[DAY 7 — PUBLIC TRANSPORT DAY // 20 JUN] 0800 HRS: Breakfast. 0930 HRS: Bus to Ximending from Jih Sun International Bank stop. Bus 252, 2 stops. Smooth. 1000 HRS: Ximending. Kids in the arcade.
