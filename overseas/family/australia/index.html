Family Log Entry #001

Tasmania: Edge of the World

Seven days. Six in a rental car grinding through Tasmania's empty highways, waterfalls, sea arches, lavender fields, and one road that literally ends at the ocean. One final day in Sydney on foot — Tower Eye, Circular Quay, Opera House, Harbour Bridge, Chinatown — all covered in a single evening before the flight home. May 2015.

// Phase 01 — Tasmania
DAY 111 May (Mon) · 175km
Hobart · Mount Wellington · Sea Lights at Night

Arrive Hobart at 1:55PM. Car collected and lunch sorted by 2:55PM — a tight window, but Tasmania is small enough that you don't waste time sitting in traffic getting out of the airport. Check in at Wrest Point Hotel by 3:30PM, bags down, and back in the car by 4:30PM.

Mount Wellington is the first real stop — the mountain that sits directly behind Hobart. Reach the summit by 5:10PM. The drive up is as good as the view from the top: a sealed road that winds through eucalyptus forest and then breaks into open dolerite rock near the peak. At 1,270 metres the wind is a different proposition from the city below. On a clear May evening, Hobart spreads out across the Derwent River valley — it's the kind of view that sets the tone for the whole trip.

Leave Wellington at 6:30PM and head east toward Brown River / Howrah / Lower Sandy Bay — the objective is the lights of the sea at night. This is an after-dark photography and atmosphere stop: the Derwent estuary at night, city lights reflected across the water, virtually no one else around. Arrive 7PM, leave by 8PM, back at Wrest Point by 8:20PM.

// Driver note: Day 1 is light on distance (175km) but you're still running on arrival energy. Mount Wellington before dark is the priority — don't let check-in drag.

DAY 212 May (Tue) · 252km
Salamanca · Richmond Bridge · Port Arthur · Tasman Peninsula · Arch · Tessellated Pavement

Full day, earliest start. Salamanca Market at 10AM — a 10-minute drive from Wrest Point. The market runs 8AM–3PM on Saturdays only, so if you're here on a Saturday this is mandatory. Fresh produce, local art, Tasmanian produce stalls, and the historic sandstone warehouses of Salamanca Place as the backdrop. Leave by 11AM.

Important: Salamanca Market is Saturday only, 8AM–3PM. If Day 2 falls on a different day, skip it and add time to Port Arthur instead.

Richmond Bridge at 11:30AM — Australia's oldest intact bridge, built by convicts in 1823. It's about a 30-minute drive northeast of Hobart. Ten minutes here is enough to walk the bridge and understand the scale of what was built with convict labour, but the surrounding village is worth a brief explore too.

Leave by noon for Port Arthur — Tasmania's most significant convict heritage site. Arrive 1:30PM. The UNESCO-listed ruins of the former penal colony occupy a peninsula facing a protected bay. Allow at least an hour — the grounds are large, the history is dense, and the guided components are worth it. The story of the place is as dark as the architecture.

Afternoon circuit of the Tasman Peninsula: leave Port Arthur at 2:30PM, reach Tasman Peninsula viewpoints at 3PM, then to Tasman Arch at 4PM — a natural stone arch carved by the sea, accessible via a short boardwalk trail. Then Tessellated Pavement at 5PM — a naturally fractured rock platform at the waterline that looks machine-cut. The geometry of it is genuinely hard to believe is natural. Head back to Hobart by 5:30PM, home by 7PM.

// Food note: Buy ready-made food before leaving Hobart. Some stretches of the Tasman Peninsula have no food options. Don't assume you'll find a café between Port Arthur and the arch.

DAY 313 May (Wed) · 357km
Russell Falls · Drive North · Launceston · Bridestowe Lavender Estate

Check out of Hobart. The new base is Elphin Villa Serviced Apartments in Launceston — but check-in is only available after 2PM, so the morning is built around the drive north with a stop on the way.

Russell Falls at 11:30AM — inside Mount Field National Park, about 75km northwest of Hobart. A short 20-minute walk from the carpark brings you to one of Tasmania's most photographed waterfalls: tiered, mossy, surrounded by tree ferns and myrtle forest. The kind of place that looks like it was designed to be impressive, except it's entirely natural and entirely free. Don't rush it.

Proceed to Launceston, checking in at Elphin Villa by 3PM. Then the afternoon race to Bridestowe Lavender Estate — Australia's largest lavender farm, about 30 minutes east of Launceston. It closes at 5PM. Arrive by 4PM to get a full hour. In May the lavender isn't in bloom (peak is December–January) but the estate is still worth visiting for the shop, the dried lavender, and the landscape. If you're planning around peak bloom, adjust timing accordingly.

// Hotel note: Elphin Villa check-in after 2PM only. Don't arrive early expecting to drop bags.

DAY 414 May (Thu) · 621km
Cataract Gorge · Edge of the World · Arthur River

The big one. 621km in a single day — the longest drive of the whole trip by a significant margin. The spreadsheet explicitly notes to observe the previous day's actual timing and reconsider the Edge of the World based on real pace. That's the right call: this day lives or dies by an early departure.

Leave at 9AM. First stop: Cataract Gorge Reserve, 30 minutes from Launceston. The gorge cuts right through the edge of the city — the South Esk River carved it over millennia, and the result is a dramatic rocky chasm with a swimming pool, chairlift, and walking tracks at its heart. Leave by 11:30AM.

Edge of the World at Arthur River — this is the headline. Drive northwest across Tasmania's remote interior (roughly 3 hours of driving). Arthur River is a small township on the northwest coast where the Southern Ocean stretches uninterrupted all the way to South America. There's a boulder on the bank with a plaque that says exactly that: the next landmass west is Argentina. Stand there. Appreciate what "edge of the world" actually feels like when there's nothing between you and it. A wish can reportedly be made here. Arrive approximately 2:30PM.

West of Arthur River: open Southern Ocean, nothing until South America. Some places earn the name. This one does.

Leave at 3:30PM and begin the long drive back east. Return to Launceston hotel by 7:30PM. Four hours of driving back after a full day's exploring — factor that in when you're standing at the water's edge deciding whether to stay another ten minutes.

// This day is the one most likely to run over time. The drive back from Arthur River is remote with limited fuel stops. Fill the tank before heading west.

DAY 515 May (Fri) · 324km
Drive South · The Neck Lookout · Bruny Island Ferry

Check out of Launceston at 10AM. Drive south back to Hobart — a different base for the final night. Check in at Bay View Villa near Hobart by 1PM, then the afternoon activity is the ferry crossing.

The Neck — a narrow isthmus connecting North and South Bruny Island, accessed via ferry from Kettering (about 30 minutes south of Hobart). The Neck Lookout is a short boardwalk climb that puts you directly above the isthmus with ocean on both sides. In May you have a reasonable chance of seeing little penguins returning to their burrows at dusk on the beach below.

Depart hotel at 2PM, reach the Kettering ferry by 2:30PM. Cross to Bruny Island, reach The Neck by 3:30PM. Leave by 4:30PM, back to the ferry by 5PM, reach the Hobart-side mainland by 5:30PM, hotel by 6PM. The evening is open — if the sea-light photography from Day 1 was unsatisfying, Brown River / Howrah / Lower Sandy Bay is the backup run. Same route, same objective.

DAY 616 May (Sat) · 21km to airport
Check Out · Return Car · Fly to Sydney · Full Sydney Afternoon

Final Tasmania morning. Check out, eat, drive the 21km to Hobart Airport. Car returned at 11:15AM — five days and roughly 1,575km covered. Check in and prepare to fly by 11:45AM.

Qantas to Sydney: departs 12:45PM, arrives Sydney Domestic at 2:35PM. From the domestic terminal, the Service Desk is opposite Baggage Carousel 3 (or Carousel 5). Transit to the hotel by train: Wolli Creek Station, T8 Green line towards Airport/City, drop off at St James Station. Reach Quality Hotel CKS Sydney by 3:30PM.

// Phase 02 — Sydney (One Day, No Car)

One afternoon and evening. No car — everything on public transport and on foot. The route is tight, the timing is deliberate, and it covers the city's five most iconic spots in a single run. The logic is simple: you've been driving remote Tasmanian roads for five days. One evening of pure city-walking is the right way to close the trip.

DAY 716 May (Sat) — Sydney Evening
Tower Eye · Circular Quay · Opera House · Harbour Bridge · Chinatown

Leave the hotel at 4PM. Walk 3 minutes to Sydney Tower Eye — arrive 4:35PM. The observation deck at 268 metres sits at the top of the city's tallest structure. Clear day and you can see all the way to the Blue Mountains. Worth the entry; the revolving restaurant is in the same building if dinner is the plan.

Walk 3 minutes to Market Street, then train from St James Station to Circular Quay — one stop, five minutes. Arrive Circular Quay at 5:30PM. Walk 10 minutes to Sydney Opera House — reach it by 6PM. You don't need to go inside. Walking up to the forecourt at dusk with the harbour behind it is the experience. The building from the outside in the evening light does everything a building should do.

From Circular Quay: Ferry from Wharf 5 to Milsons Point. Cross the harbour directly under the bridge. Step off and walk to the base of Sydney Harbour Bridge — reach it by 6:30PM. The city skyline from the north shore at night is the best view of the trip's final day. Take the time.

Train back into the city: Milsons Point Station to Town Hall Station — two stops. Arrive Chinatown at 7PM. Dinner. The area runs from Haymarket down through Dixon Street — duck noodles, BBQ pork, and the kind of late-night food energy that's a sharp contrast to five days of remote Tasmanian silence. Back to Quality Hotel CKS via Bus 422 (26 stops from Chinatown Centre to West Botany Street at Flora Street) by 8:30PM.

Next day, 3:55PM: Emirates/Qantas departs Sydney. Singapore arrival 10:15PM. Trip closed.

Tasmania Driver Notes

  • Day 4 is the planning anchor. 621km in one day means an early departure, a full tank, and a realistic exit time from Arthur River. Don't still be at the Edge of the World at 4PM — you won't make it back before dark.
  • Sunset at 5PM throughout. May is late autumn in Tasmania. You lose light fast. Every afternoon activity has a hard cutoff. Build your schedule around that, not around Singapore daylight assumptions.
  • No food between stops in remote areas. Specifically on Day 2 (Tasman Peninsula) and Day 4 (Arthur River run) — buy food in Hobart or Launceston before heading out. Do not assume a café exists where you're going.
  • Fuel before heading west on Day 4. The Arthur River route crosses through some of the least-populated parts of Tasmania. Don't gamble on finding a petrol station mid-way.
  • Salamanca Market is Saturday only. If Day 2 doesn't fall on a Saturday, the market isn't there. Adjust accordingly.
  • Bridestowe lavender peak is December–January. A May visit gets the estate and the shop but not the purple fields. If you specifically want the lavender in bloom, this is a summer trip.
